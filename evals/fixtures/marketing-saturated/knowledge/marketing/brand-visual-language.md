# Visual language — quiet, warm, photographic

## What this is

The high-level visual identity Lumera operates within. This is not a comprehensive style guide — it's the principles a designer holds when making any visual decision. Detailed tokens (colors, type ramps, spacing) live in the brand book PDF (workspace/marketing/), not here.

## Principles

- **Quiet over loud.** Negative space, restrained palettes. The product is the hero; the design recedes.
- **Warm over clinical.** Earth tones (cream, sage, terracotta) over the cold-pharmaceutical white-and-blue category default.
- **Photographic over illustrative.** Real product, real hands, real light. Illustrations only as supporting elements (data viz, instructional steps), never as primary brand imagery.

## What this looks like in practice

- Hero imagery is shot on muted natural backgrounds, never lightbox-white.
- Type pairing: a humanist serif (display) + a low-contrast sans (body). No geometric sans.
- Color: ~70% cream / off-white surfaces, ~20% earth tones, ~10% accent. The accent rotates seasonally.

## What it isn't

- A maximalist, layered, "everything is a sticker" aesthetic. We are not Glossier or Liquid Death.
- A retro / nostalgic gimmick. Quiet is not vintage.
