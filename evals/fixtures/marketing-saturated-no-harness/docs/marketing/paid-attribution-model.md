# Attribution model — first-touch primary, last-touch secondary

## Model

We score paid spend on a **first-touch primary, last-touch secondary** basis with a 30-day window. Both numbers ship in the weekly growth dashboard.

## Why two numbers

- First-touch tells us which channels actually *open the door* (cold awareness). Without first-touch credit, top-of-funnel channels (Meta video, TikTok) look weak even when they're carrying the load.
- Last-touch tells us which channels close the loop. Critical for branded Search performance specifically.
- Reporting both in parallel avoids the "everything looks like a Google PMax win" trap of single-attribution last-touch defaults.

## Window

30 days. Lumera's purchase consideration cycle for a new customer averages 11 days; the 30-day window covers the long tail and matches the LTV calculation period.

## What we don't do

- Multi-touch / fractional attribution. Tried in Q1 2026, found it expensive to build and impossible to communicate to non-technical stakeholders. Two clear numbers > one fuzzy one.
- Post-purchase brand-lift surveys for paid attribution. Useful for brand work, not for paid optimization.
